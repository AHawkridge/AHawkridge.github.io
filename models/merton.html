Merton Jump Diffusion

Geometric Brownian Motion with a Compound Poisson Process

One of the first models to capture the the volatility smile was the Merton Jump Diffusion model (MJD), it was first introduced by R. Merton in the aim to extend the Black-Scholes model to remove some of its assumptions. The model states that the underlying assets have occasional large discontinuous jumps in price while still following the normal GBM. These large jumps are infrequently observed in the market, but would not be possible to model with just GBM, Merton’s model integrates these jumps into the price dynamics of the underlying asset, providing a more complete description of the underlying assets behavior. The following stochastic differential equation is proposed to describe these asset price jumps: \[ S_t = S_0 e^{(\mu - \frac{\sigma^2}{2} - \lambda k)t + \sigma W_t +\sum_{i=1}^{N_t} J_i} \] The additional summation is the compound Poisson process, it is the result of all the jumps that occur over the stock path. The additional λk term comes from the Poisson distribution and ensures that the drift rate is not effected by the inclusion of jumps .
